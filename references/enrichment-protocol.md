# Three-Source Enrichment Protocol

Every topic identified in Step 2 must be enriched from three independent sources — one per cognitive layer — before it can be considered ready for the content map. Missing any one source leaves the topic incomplete.

| Source | Layer | What it provides |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Social media | Resonance | Real people saying the same thing — makes listeners feel "that's exactly me" |
| Academic research | Credibility | A specific finding with numbers — gives the phenomenon a quantified grounding |
| Sociology / psychology theory | Explanation | A named concept — gives listeners the "aha, that's why" frame |

The three sources are independent — they do not need to converge on a single conclusion. Breadth first: each source supplies raw material from its own dimension. The host will find the connections naturally during recording. Your job is to have all three dimensions ready, not to synthesize them in advance.

---

## Source 1 — Social Media (Resonance Layer)

**Goal**: Find real people saying the same thing in their own words. Not news coverage, not expert commentary — ordinary people expressing a feeling that makes listeners say "that's exactly me."

**Search directions:**
```
web_search("[topic keyword] reddit experience personal")
web_search("[topic keyword] forum discussion people share")
web_search("[topic keyword] twitter community reaction")
```

**What to find**: A specific post, comment, or story with concrete detail and emotional texture — not vague generalities. Bonus if it shows how widespread the feeling is (upvotes, reply count, shares). Prioritize expressions that name a feeling people have but rarely articulate.

**Does not count**: News quotes, expert interviews, brand content, or any comment without specific detail.

---

## Source 2 — Academic Research (Credibility Layer)

**Goal**: Find a specific research finding that gives the topic's phenomenon a quantified or experimental grounding. Not "research shows X matters" — but "a [year] study found that under [condition], X occurred at [rate]."

**Search directions:**
```
web_search("[topic phenomenon] study research findings journal")
web_search("[topic phenomenon] psychology experiment results")
web_search("[topic phenomenon] academic paper conclusion")
```

**What to find**: A conclusion with specific numbers or experimental design. Counter-intuitive findings are best — "even under X condition, Y still happened." Source must be credible (peer-reviewed journal, university research, major institution report).

**Does not count**: "Research shows stress affects people" — no specific condition, no number. Studies older than 10 years unless foundational. Unsourced "studies show" claims.

**Good example**: "A 2020 study in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology found that when people imagined an unchosen option as 'still available,' satisfaction with their actual choice dropped 23% — even when the unchosen option was objectively worse. The mere existence of an option erodes your experience of what you have."

**Bad example**: "Barry Schwartz's *The Paradox of Choice* says more options means less satisfaction." — textbook-level common knowledge; listeners can Google it themselves.

---

## Source 3 — Sociology / Psychology Theory (Explanatory Framework Layer)

**Goal**: Find a named theory or concept that gives the topic's phenomenon an "aha, that's why" explanatory frame. Not to show off — to make listeners feel "this thing has structure; it's not just my personal chaos."

**Search directions:**
```
web_search("[topic phenomenon] psychology theory concept name")
web_search("[topic phenomenon] sociology framework explain why")
web_search("[topic feeling] cognitive bias theory named")
```

**What to find**: A named concept (FOBO, Zeigarnik effect, projective idealization, cognitive dissonance…) that explains *why* the phenomenon happens — not just labels it. Prefer concepts the audience hasn't heard but will immediately understand over well-worn ones like "confirmation bias."

**Usage rule**: Theory is scaffolding, not a display case. One theory per topic, maximum. Use it to help the host explain the phenomenon, then put it down.

---

## Output Format

For each topic, record your enrichment findings before moving to the content map:

```
Topic: "[Precise topic title]"

Source 1 — Social media:
  Platform: [reddit / twitter / forum / etc.]
  Finding: [specific post or comment with detail]
  Why it resonates: [what feeling does it name?]

Source 2 — Academic research:
  Study: [author, year, journal/institution]
  Finding: [specific result with number or condition]
  Counter-intuitive angle: [what most people don't know]

Source 3 — Theory:
  Concept: [named theory or concept]
  Explains: [why the phenomenon happens, in one sentence]
  How to use it: [one sentence on how the host introduces it without showing off]
```
